2: Ghana Communication Technology University

Established in November 2005, GCTU was granted accreditation by the National Accreditation Board (NAB) on March 30, 2006 and officially inaugurated on August 15, 2006. The University College is governed by a nine-member University Council, supported by the President, Registrar, Deans of Faculties and staff

  • Location: Tesano
  • Population: 1,567
  • Region: Greater Accra
Ghana Communication Technology University

3: University of Ghana

3. The University of Ghana, the premier university in Ghana, was founded as the University College of the Gold Coast by Ordinance on August 11, 1948 for the purpose of providing and promoting university education, learning and research. To become a world class-research-intensive University.

  • Location: Legon
  • Population: 300,000
  • Region: Greater Accra
University of Ghana

4: University of Cape Coast

Following the report of the International Commission on University Education in Ghana (May 1961), the government of Osagyefo Kwame Nkrumah established the University College of Cape Coast (now the University of Cape Coast) and formally inaugurated it on 15th December, 1962.

  • Location: Cape Coast
  • Population: 5,785
  • Region: Central
University of Cape Coast
